# Walkthrough - FASTA Alignment Helper

I have implemented the helper functions to align FASTA chunks to the genomic reference.

## Changes

### `utils/FASTA_analyzer.py`

Added methods for global alignment, exon matching, coverage stats, and **variant identification**.

## Final Logic Summary

Here is the finalized algorithm for identifying alleles from FASTA chunks:

1.  **Exon Alignment (All-vs-All)**:
    - The sequence is aligned against **all individual exons (1-7)**.
    - **Orientation Check**: Both **Forward** and **Reverse Complement** are tested for every exon.
    - The best matching exon/orientation is selected based on Alignment Score.

2.  **Coverage & Similarity Calculation**:
    - **Coverage %**: Percentage of the *exon sequence* that is covered by the read.
    - **Similarity %**: Percentage of matching bases within the aligned region.

3.  **Two-Step Confirmation Filter**:
    - A match is only considered **"Confirmed"** if:
        - **Coverage > 80%** (To ensure the read covers most of the exon).
        - **Similarity > 90%** (To ensure it's a high-quality match, allowing for a few SNPs).
    - Matches below these thresholds are treated as noise/unmapped.

4.  **Variant Calling**:
    - For confirmed matches, the sequence is re-aligned to the specific exon in the correct orientation.
    - **SNPs**, **Insertions**, and **Deletions** are identified and mapped to genomic positions.

5.  **Allele Identification**:
    - The identified variants are looked up in the `ABO_enhanced_subgraph.gml`.
    - Returns a list of **Possible Alleles** (e.g., `ABO*B.01`, `ABO*O.01.01`) that contain these variants.
    - If no variants are found (Perfect Match), it defaults to the Reference Allele (e.g., A1.01).
